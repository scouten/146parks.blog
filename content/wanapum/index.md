This morning I visited Wanapum Recreation Area on the shores of the Columbia River.

<!-- more -->

I arrived at sunrise, thinking that the sun would lend extra color and vibrance to the scenery. It did.

{{ es_cdn_image(id = "es-4690-018", cdn_version = "v1", cdn_key = "img/v1/2024/09/es-4690-018", alt = "A wide pre-dawn view across a calm lake or reservoir, with low rolling hills silhouetted on the far shore beneath a sky glowing soft orange and pink fading to pale blue. The site offers about 50 tent and RV camp sites nestled amongst trees along the river. So as not to disturb anyone, I took my photo of the campground from the highway above.

{{ es_cdn_image(id = "es-4690-021", cdn_version = "v1", cdn_key = "img/v1/2024/09/es-4690-021", alt = "A wide, hazy view across a calm river or reservoir at dawn, with dry sagebrush and golden grass in the foreground, low rolling hills and basalt cliffs in the distance, and a soft pink-and-blue sky overhead. A small settlement with trees and buildings sits along the far shoreline to the right.") }}

Once in Wanapum, I walked around the picnic grounds and beaches, which I had almost entirely to myself.
